The Club

Club History

Founded 1932

The First Minutes Were Taken by P.G. Wodehouse

Hollywood Cricket Club was founded by Sir C. Aubrey “Round the Corner” Smith. The Club’s By-Laws were passed on January 20, 1932 and approved February 18, 1932.

Sir C. Aubrey Smith

Sir Aubrey Smith was born July 21, 1863 in St. Stephen’s Hospital. As time went by he became a serious soccer player, but his real desire was to play cricket.

An opening brisk fast-medium bowler, he played for Charterhouse, Cambridge, Sussex and England. He debuted in 1882 for Sussex County Cricket Club and served as Captain in 1887, 1888, and 1890. He also captained two England Touring Sides — to Australia and South Africa.

He went to Hollywood in 1929 and, alongside Boris Karloff, taught cricket at UCLA in 1932. That same year, Arthur Mailey’s Australian team captained by Vic Richardson played Smith’s team of British actors at UCLA — Smith scored 24 runs against Don Bradman’s team.

The publicity from that tour was used to found the Hollywood Cricket Club and secure a permanent ground at Griffith Park in Burbank, officially opened on May 23, 1933 with four pitches.

Created a C.B.E. in 1938 at Buckingham Palace, he received a knighthood in 1944. Sir Charles Aubrey Smith died in Beverly Hills on December 20, 1948. He will always be remembered by Hollywood Cricket Club.

Notable Members

Over the decades, Hollywood CC attracted many prominent names from the entertainment world who shared a love of the game.

The Ground

Sir Aubrey named his house at 2881 Coldwater Canyon Drive, near Mulholland Drive, “The Round Corner” — a nod to his famous bowling style. The club’s permanent ground at Griffith Park, Burbank was officially opened May 23, 1933.

The Club

Rodney Soher Cup

Named for Mr. Rodney Soher, this cup is annually awarded to the person who best exemplifies the spirit of the game. First awarded in 1955 by actor Edmund Gwenn — a great personal friend of Sir Aubrey — in England. Ever since, it has been awarded by secret ballot.
